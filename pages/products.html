Distinguishing Features of FPC

1.FPC (Fiber Particulate Composite), also named as "organic plastics" in Japan, is a bioplastic particle consiting of natural plant fiber and starches in specific proportion. Plant fibers in FPC come from varous types of agricultural by-products or residues, such as rice husks, wheat husks, corn stalks, sugarcane bagasse, palm, coconut shells, wood chips, coffee residues, etc.

2.Production process of FPC is purely GREEN, none chemicals added, none ollution generated. The only by-product is clean water.

3.Compared to the most popular bioplastic material up to date - PLA (Poly Lactic Acid), FPC stself is 100% hydro-degradable with neither pollution nor txic generated in degradation process, while PLA generates methane into the atmosphere during degradaton, unavailable to mix with other types of plastic materials, costs high in production and prices high in the market (US$2.70/Kg). To reduce global waring, it's a must for many countries to limit or ban on using PLA in the very near future.

Application of FPC

1.Mixed with (1) plastics - PP, PE,or EVA foam; or (2) rubber - natural or synthetic SBR, TPR, TPE, FPC makes perfect composite and takes the amount of 30% to 70% in total material. That is, using FPC will directly reduce the amount of plstic or rubber material. In addition, products made of FPC is recyclable and reusable. Adapting to the global ban on plastics, FPC is and will be the best solution.
